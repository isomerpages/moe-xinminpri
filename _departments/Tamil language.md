<h2><strong>Vision</strong></h2>
<p>Be an effective communicator who is a self-directed learner and one who
appreciates Tamil language and culture.</p>
<h2><strong>Mission</strong></h2>
<p>Every Tamil Language student in Xinmin Primary to have a strong foundation
in the language and culture.</p>
<h4><strong>Key Programmes</strong></h4>
<p>Xinmin’s Tamil Language (TL) curriculum is designed to build a strong
foundation in both language and culture, in line with our mission, while
nurturing students to become confident communicators and self-directed
learners. Through a range of purposeful and engaging programmes, students
are given opportunities to develop their oral and written communication
skills, take ownership of their learning, and deepen their appreciation
of Tamil language and culture in meaningful contexts.</p>
<p><strong>Speech and Drama (Primary 1 &amp; 2)</strong>
</p>
<p>The Speech and Drama programme provides Primary 1 and 2 students with
authentic opportunities to express themselves confidently in Tamil through
role-play, storytelling and performance. By engaging in these activities
from a young age, students build a strong foundation in speaking and listening
skills, while developing confidence as effective communicators. At the
same time, they begin to take ownership of their learning as they explore
language creatively and develop an early appreciation for Tamil expression
and storytelling.</p>

<p><strong>Language Booster Games</strong>
</p>
<p>The Language Booster Games Workshop is designed to strengthen students’
foundation in Tamil vocabulary and language use through interactive and
engaging activities. As students explore new words and phrases in a meaningful
way, they become more confident in thinking and communicating in Tamil.
The programme also nurtures self-directed learning by encouraging students
to actively apply what they have learnt in different contexts, while fostering
a growing interest and appreciation for the language.</p>
<p><strong>Media Communication</strong>
</p>
<p>The Media Communication Workshop provides students with opportunities
to develop their spoken Tamil through script-based role-play and re-enactment
activities. Through these collaborative experiences, students strengthen
their ability to communicate ideas clearly and effectively, while learning
to adapt their language for different purposes and audiences. This programme
supports students in becoming confident communicators and encourages them
to take initiative in refining their language use through practice and
peer interaction.</p>
<p><strong>Tamil Newspaper Subscription</strong>
</p>
<p>The introduction of Tamil newspapers provides students with authentic
exposure to current affairs, helping them build a strong foundation in
reading and comprehension. Through class discussions and presentations,
students learn to express their perspectives clearly while being open to
diverse viewpoints. This programme develops them into informed and reflective
communicators, while nurturing empathy and a deeper appreciation of the
relevance of Tamil language in real-world contexts.</p>
<p><strong>MT SOAR Reading Programme (Primary 1 to 3)</strong>
</p>
<p>Primary 1 to 3 students engage in dedicated reading and library sessions
using age-appropriate supplementary readers, alongside digital resources
such as Little Readers on the Student Learning Space (SLS) platform. As
they explore a variety of texts, students develop curiosity by asking questions
and making connections to their own experiences. Regular reading routines
both in school and at home encourage them to take responsibility for their
learning, while discussions with teachers and parents support them in applying
new vocabulary and ideas in different contexts. This programme also strengthens
the partnership between home and school through meaningful shared reading
experiences.</p>
<p><strong>Reading Programme (Primary 4 to 6)</strong>
</p>
<p>Primary 4 to 6 students participate in structured reading activities,
including school library visits and dedicated class reading sessions. Through
these experiences, students deepen their understanding of Tamil texts and
build confidence in expressing their thoughts. The use of reading cards
and recognition through reading badges motivate students to take ownership
of their progress, while cultivating a sustained interest in reading and
a stronger appreciation of Tamil language and literature.</p>
<p><strong>Cultural Programmes (Primary 3 &amp; 5)</strong>
</p>
<p>Our cultural programmes provide students with immersive opportunities
to experience and appreciate Tamil culture, supporting the development
of a strong foundation in both language and cultural understanding.</p>
<p>Through Primary 3 fortnightly activities and the Primary 5 Cultural Camp,
students participate in hands-on experiences such as Kolam art, pot painting
and traditional games. These activities enable students to use the language
in authentic contexts while interacting and collaborating with their peers.
As they engage in these meaningful experiences, students deepen their appreciation
of Tamil traditions and values, while developing confidence in communicating
and expressing themselves.</p>
<p><strong>School-Based Competitions</strong>
</p>
<p>School-based competitions provide valuable platforms for students to demonstrate
their learning and showcase their talents in various aspects of Tamil language
and cultural expression. Through activities such as singing, role-playing,
essay writing and digital projects, students strengthen their communication
skills and express their ideas creatively. These experiences encourage
students to take initiative in their learning, build confidence in their
abilities, and develop a deeper appreciation for the richness of Tamil
language and culture.</p>
