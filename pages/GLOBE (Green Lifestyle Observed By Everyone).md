<h1><strong>G.L.O.B.E (Green Lifestyle Observed By Everyone)</strong></h1>
<h1><strong>Vision:</strong></h1>
<p>Nurturing Every Xinminite to Be an Environmental Steward for Life</p>
<h1><strong>Mission:</strong></h1>
<p>To broaden and deepen Xinminites’ environmental knowledge, and inculcate
a sense of responsibility in making positive environmental efforts</p>
<h4><strong>Signature Programmes</strong></h4>
<p>The Environmental Science Education Applied Learning Programme at XMPS
has been carefully curated and executed to equip students to be lifelong
learners and do their part to alleviate environmental problems.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Hydroponics Farming</strong>
<br>Innovative and sustainable farming methods such as hydroponics pique students'
curiosity about plant reproduction and environmental science as they learn
to grow their own food using non-traditional soilless methods. Through
hands-on experience in planting, transplanting and crop harvesting, students
gain practical knowledge that they are inspired to share with their families,
extending their learning beyond the classroom.
<br>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>GrowFridge Farming</strong>
<br>Students experience growing herbs and vegetables using innovative technology.
The micro-fridge device is Singapore’s first plug-and-play smart micro-farm,
designed like a kitchen appliance that allows students and teachers to
grow their own microgreens under controlled climatic conditions in an indoor
setting. Students not only gain comprehensive knowledge of healthy living
but also learn about urban farming technology, including temperature settings,
watering cycles and lighting for optimal plant growth.
<br>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Aeroponics Vertical Farming</strong>
<br>Students learn aeroponics, a plant cultivation technique where roots hang
suspended in air whilst nutrient solution is delivered through mist. The
nutrient-rich solution is sprayed or dripped directly onto the plant roots,
eliminating the need for soil. Through this aeroponic urban farming system,
students discover how plants can be grown vertically, requiring significantly
less space than traditional farming whilst being more efficient and environmentally
sustainable.
<br>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Veggie Pod Farming</strong>
<br>By growing vegetables in raised and covered garden beds, students learn
that farming can be carried out efficiently in a controlled environment,
away from common garden pests. The installation of a self-watering system
allows for even greater efficiency in this method of farming. Through fun,
hands-on experiences of planting, transplanting and crop harvesting, students
are inspired to share both their harvest and urban farming experiences
with their families.
<br>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Organic Veggie Tower Farming</strong>
<br>Students take charge of their learning and develop responsibility in caring
for the vegetables. As the Veggie Tower replicates a natural ecosystem
and converts kitchen scraps into natural fertiliser for the vegetables,
this programme inspires students to learn more about decomposition processes
and plant growth.</p>
</li>
</ul>
